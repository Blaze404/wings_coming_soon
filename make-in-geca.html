What is MAKE IN GECA?

MAKE IN GECA is an event where students, designers and hackers collaborate for limited time to deliver solutions to various challenges proposed by industries and need-knowers. The solutions prepared are hardware that are ready to be launched in market helping to gather investors for start-up.

  • The team size will be maximum 4 participants
  • The registration fee is 250 per team
  • The teams will be provided with the problem statements on the WINGS 2020 website, they need to choose ANY ONE problem statement.
  • The teams should engineer a hardware prototype of their choice to best fit the solution of the chosen problem statement and must be same as stated in abstract. The abstract should be submitted before deadline which will also be declared on the WINGS 2020 website
  • The team member must be present with their registration receipt along with college ID and government proof
  • The lists of machinery which will be provided by us are given below. The machinery would be handed to the team who are capable and knows how to use them. If anything goes wrong during the manufacturing it’s completely the team’s responsibility
  • Apart from this other necessary things required for the prototype should be arranged by the team themselves
  • Rules may be changed without prior notice. In case of any disputes, the decision of the organizers and the judges will be final and binding to all.
  • The teams would be provided with meal and breakfast along with some refreshment twice during the event.

Rules.

Round 1

The teams willing to participate will have to choose one of the problem statements provided to them. The teams need to submit the abstract in the form of PowerPoint presentation with maximum 25 slides till the deadline. The presentation should include the prototype idea, manufacturing process, manufacturing tools, total cost and other relevant factors.

Round 2

Once submission of abstract is done the selected teams will be called to implement their prototype in the given timespan of 24 hrs. The prototype build will undergo tests and a final presentation given to judges.
